# Product

## Register

product

## Users

StreamCoreTV is for subscribers using Android mobile, tablet, and TV surfaces to sign in, choose or manage profiles, browse a VOD catalogue, continue
saved content, discover recommendations, and open content details. Usage spans handheld touch interaction and 10-foot TV viewing with D-pad
navigation.

## Product Purpose

StreamCoreTV is a Compose-first, backend-agnostic Android streaming app family. It provides a reusable client-specific VOD foundation where core,
domain, and feature UI remain independent of provider SDKs, DTOs, API responses, and client-specific models. Success means the app feels like a
polished consumer streaming product while preserving clean multi-module boundaries and provider-swappable data implementations.

## Brand Personality

Premium, cinematic, calm. The interface should be content-led and familiar to streaming users without cloning Netflix, Disney+, HBO Max, Prime Video,
or any provider-specific identity.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS dashboards, marketing landing-page composition, loud orange-and-black demo styling, white or cream page backgrounds for streaming
surfaces, exact competitor cloning, social-feed layouts, device mockups inside the app UI, busy chrome, tiny TV controls, subtle TV focus rings, and
provider-specific branding in shared or feature UI.

## Design Principles

1. Content carries the experience: posters, backdrops, rows, details, and profile imagery should create the cinematic feel before brand color does.
2. Earn streaming familiarity: use category-standard navigation, carousel, row, details, profile, and login patterns, but keep visual identity
   original and client-neutral.
3. Preserve backend agnosticism: shared product UI should depend on app models, UI state, actions, and effects, never provider DTOs or SDK concepts.
4. Fit the platform: mobile and tablet prioritize touch ergonomics and adaptive density; TV prioritizes 10-foot readability, deterministic D-pad
   focus, and large focus states.
5. Keep states production-ready: loading, empty, offline, error, unauthenticated, entitlement-blocked, and long-localized-text states should feel
   designed, not bolted on.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Prioritize readable contrast, scalable text, reduced-motion-safe animations, and clear TV focus states. Shared UI should remain usable across mobile,
tablet, and TV input models without relying on color alone to communicate state.
